Education

India Education Initiatives

We’re using our resources and software expertise to improve global learning. Currently, in India alone, over 200 million people are unable to read or write. Advances in mobile computing, internet access, and AI technology, however, are creating new opportunities to improve access to education and improve living conditions.

Foundation for Excellence

We established Foundation for Excellence in 2009. Since then, Foundation for Excellence has built classrooms and provided furniture, blackboards, science lab equipment, and drinking water to over 25,000 students in India. The foundation has also funded a school promoting STEM for girls and overseen the construction of five computer labs.

Delivering Mobile Learning Apps

MAQ Software has also partnered with Global Learning XPRIZE, a $15 million prize fund awarded to the team that develops the best educational app for children in Africa, and two of the largest education nonprofits in India, Pratham and Ekal, to deliver learning apps to the Indian subcontinent.

What’s the benefit?

The Global Learning XPRIZE finalists field-tested their apps in Africa for over a year, and results were overwhelmingly positive. Students who used one of the apps—Kitkit School—showed an average reading test improvement of 30–45% and an average math test improvement of 14–37%. Students who did not use the app showed much lower or no change in their test scores.

Here’s how it works:

The Global Learning XPRIZE challenge began in 2014. The Global Learning XPRIZE committee narrowed the pool of candidates down to five finalists. Eligibility requirements ensured that all Global Learning XPRIZE apps were open source. MAQ Software localized the finalists’ apps into Hindi and, with the help of Pratham and Ekal, is delivering the apps to low-income communities in India. Access to the apps will be ensured by the distribution of mobile phones with the apps already installed.

Research Fellows

MAQ Software has partnered with faculty members of leading IITs to improve learning outcomes for millions of Indian students using advances in AI. MAQ Software provides three-year grants for these fellows to sustain their research and promote the adoption of technology for educational instruction.
