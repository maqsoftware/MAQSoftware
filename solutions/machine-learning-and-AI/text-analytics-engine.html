Want to get started? Use the following guide to gain quick insights into your data.

Figure 1: Get Started Page


  1. Open the Text Analytics Engine.

  2. Sign yourself into the portal by providing the necessary consent and clicking “I Agree”.

  3. Click “Get Started” – you will be directed to the Data Connect page

    Figure 2: Data Connect page


  4. On the Data Connect page, choose any of the following text input options:
    • Upload Your Data: upload and analyze your own text (the engine supports xlsx and csv)
    • Use Sample Data: for a quick look at how the tool works, use one of our sample datasets
    • Enter Your Text: type or copy your text directly into the text box (use the “Enter” key as a delimiter)

      Figure 3: Data Selection page


  5. Once you have chosen your text input, you will be directed to the Data Selection page.
    • If you input your own data, select the data category from the dropdown. If your data is event-related, the visualizations will be different than the visualizations for “Other Data”. Select the option most relevant to your data.
    • Next, select the column you want to analyze (if using a spreadsheet with multiple columns) and enter your desired number of topics in the left-hand dropdown.
    • If you chose a sample dataset, the left-hand dropdowns are pre-selected.
    • When you’re ready, click “Generate Insights” – you will then be directed to the Data Overview page. Depending on how large your dataset is, this may take a few minutes.

      Figure 4: Data Overview Page


  6. Once your data has loaded, you can explore key insights on the Data Overview page. From there, you can drill down into the Topic Modeling, Sentiment Analysis, and Developer Zone tabs for more specific data breakdowns. For more information on key points and visualizations, click the information icon in the top right-hand corner of each visualization.

How to Modify Topics

A topic is the subject of text content. For example, the following comments would all fall under the topic “customer service”:

  • “MAQ Software customer service is fantastic!”
  • “An MAQ Software support rep reached out right away.”
  • “I am very happy with the quality of customer service.”

    Figure 5: Rename Topics


  1. If using “Other Data”, you can rename topics by navigating to the Topic Modeling page. Scroll to the bottom of the page and click “Rename Topic”. Note: If you rename topics and re-run the model, you will no longer be able to rename topics. Instead, you can create a new topic using the same steps. If using “Event Data”, you will only be able to create topics.
    Figure 6: Topic Modeling Page - Filter Topic


  2. To filter topics, click “Filter Topic” and remove the undesired topics.
  3. When you’re happy with your topic modifications, re-run the model for updated insights.
  4. To share your insights, click “Download Insights” in the top right-hand corner of the dashboard for a PDF report of the page.
